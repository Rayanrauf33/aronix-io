## Section: What we build

Label: What we build

We build you an AI receptionist trained on your business. It knows your services, your prices, your hours, and your service area. It answers in two rings, every time, including nights, weekends, and the lunch rush.

It holds a normal conversation. It answers the common questions, handles the back-and-forth, and books the appointment directly into your calendar. Real slots only, so it can't double-book you.

When a call needs a human, it doesn't guess. It takes the caller's details, tells them someone will follow up, and texts you right away. You set the rules for what counts as urgent.

After every call you get a text summary: who called, what they wanted, what got booked. Nothing sits in voicemail. Nothing gets lost.

## Section: FAQ

Q: Will it sound robotic?
A: Listen to the demo above and judge for yourself. We tune the voice, pacing, and script to match how your front desk actually talks. Callers are informed they're speaking with an AI assistant where that's required.

Q: What happens when it can't answer something?
A: It doesn't make things up. It takes the caller's name, number, and question, tells them a human will follow up, and texts you immediately. You decide which situations get escalated straight to your phone.

Q: Do I need to change my phone number?
A: No. It runs on your existing number. We can have it answer every call, or only pick up when your team hasn't answered within a few rings.

Q: Can it double-book me or make up prices?
A: It books through your real calendar, so it can only take open slots. Prices and policies come from the information you approve during setup, and every call is recorded so anything off gets caught and fixed.

Q: How long until it's live?
A: A few weeks from kickoff to launch depending on scope — we'll confirm an exact timeline after the audit. Most of that is testing, because we don't put it on live calls until you've approved how it handles the hard ones.

Q: What if it doesn't work for my business?
A: Then we'll say so before you pay for a build. The audit exists to check whether your call volume and job value actually justify this. Sometimes the honest answer is a simple missed-call text-back instead, and that's a smaller project.
