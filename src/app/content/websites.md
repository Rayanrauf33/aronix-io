## Section: The problem

Label: Why most service websites don't convert

A website that looks professional is not the same as a website that performs. The average service business website converts between 1% and 3% of its visitors. That means if 500 people find you through Google this month, between 485 and 495 of them leave without getting in touch.

The most common reasons aren't design failures: they're structural ones. The offer isn't clear above the fold. The social proof doesn't speak to the visitor's specific problem. The call to action asks for too much commitment too early. There are three different things the website is trying to say, and none of them land.

If you're spending money on Google Ads or investing in SEO, a website that converts at 1% is wasting most of that spend.

[Stat strip:]
- 1–3%: average conversion rate for a service business website
- 2.5 seconds: time most visitors decide whether to stay or leave
- 88%: of visitors who have a bad experience won't return to the website
- 5x: higher conversion rate for landing pages with a single clear call to action

## Section: What we build

Label: What we build

We build conversion-focused websites on Next.js. Fast, clean, and structured around the decisions your customers make before they contact you.

Before we write a line of code, we look at who your customers are, what they're searching for, and what questions they need answered before they'll make contact. The structure of the site: what's on the homepage, how the services are explained, where the calls to action sit: comes from that research, not from what looks good in a template.

Every page is designed to do one job. The homepage gets the right visitor to the right service. The service pages answer the questions that typically stop people from enquiring. The contact experience is as low-friction as possible.

We also build the integrations in from the start. Chat agents, booking systems, lead capture, analytics tracking: these aren't bolt-ons. They're part of the build.

We don't hand over a finished site and leave you to figure out the copy. We write or edit the copy as part of the build, because the structure and the words have to work together.

We also don't disappear after launch. The first 60 days of a new website are when you learn the most about what's working and what isn't. We stay involved through that period and make adjustments based on real data.

## Section: FAQ

Q: Do you work with businesses that already have a website?
A: Yes. If your current website has a solid structure but conversion problems, we can audit and improve it rather than rebuild from scratch. We'll tell you which approach makes more sense after looking at your current site and traffic data.

Q: We've had websites built before and they didn't perform. Why would this be different?
A: The most common reason websites don't perform is that they're built around what the business wants to say rather than what the customer needs to hear to make contact. We start with your customer's perspective and build backwards from there. That's a different process from most web builds.

Q: Do you write the copy or do we?
A: We write or edit the copy as part of the build. You provide the information about your services, your customers, and what makes you different: we turn it into page copy that converts. You review and approve everything before it goes live.

Q: What platform do you build on?
A: Next.js. It's fast, it ranks well, and it gives you a solid technical foundation for everything that gets added later: SEO, automation integrations, dynamic content. We don't build on Wix, Squarespace, or WordPress page builders.

Q: Will we be able to update it ourselves after launch?
A: We build with a content management setup that lets you update text and images without touching code. For structural changes or new pages, most clients come back to us: but you're not locked in.

Q: How long does a build take?
A: From discovery to launch, typically a few weeks depending on scope and how quickly content reviews happen on your end — we'll confirm an exact timeline after the audit. We'll give you a timeline at the start and flag early if anything is likely to shift it.
