## Section: The problem

Label: What manual processes actually cost you

The obvious cost is time. When your team spends hours each week on data entry, status updates, report generation, approval routing, or copying information between tools, that's real capacity being spent on work that a system could do.

The less obvious cost is errors. Manual processes fail in proportion to how often they run. A step gets skipped, a field gets left blank, a notification doesn't go out. These mistakes compound quietly until they cause a problem that's expensive to fix.

The least obvious cost is scale. A business that runs on manual processes hits a ceiling. You can only hire so many people to do the same tasks faster. Automated processes don't have that ceiling.

## Section: What we build

Label: What we build

We map the processes that are draining your team's time and rebuild them as automated workflows. The work is specific to how your business actually operates, not a generic template dropped into your tools.

A typical workflow automation connects two or more of the tools you already use: your CRM, your inbox, your project management system, your accounting software: and moves information between them automatically when a trigger event happens. A new client is signed, a project moves to a new stage, an invoice is paid. The system picks it up and does the next steps without anyone initiating them manually.

We also build internal approval and notification systems. When something needs a human decision, the right person gets notified with the right context, in the tool they already use, without anyone having to chase the information.

The end result is a process that runs the same way every time, at any volume, with a clear log of what happened and when.

## Section: How it works

Label: From messy process to clean system

01: Process audit
We spend time understanding how your team actually does the work today. Where the steps are, where the handoffs happen, and where things go wrong or get dropped.

02: Map and design
We document the process as it should work and identify every integration point. You review and sign off on the design before we build anything.

03: Build and test
We build the automation and run it against real scenarios: edge cases, error conditions, high volume. We don't hand it over until it handles the hard cases cleanly.

04: Deploy and train
We deploy it into your live environment and walk your team through how to monitor it and what to do if something unexpected happens. You're not dependent on us to understand your own system.

## Section: FAQ

Q: Do I need to change the tools my team uses?
A: Not usually. We build around your existing stack wherever possible. If a tool is genuinely blocking the automation: because it has no API or integration options: we'll tell you that during the audit before any money changes hands.

Q: What happens if the automation breaks?
A: Every automation we build includes error handling and alerts. If something fails, you get a notification immediately with enough information to understand what happened. It doesn't fail silently. We also include post-launch support so we can fix issues that come up in the first weeks of live running.

Q: Can you automate a process that involves someone making a decision partway through?
A: Yes. We build decision gates into workflows: points where the automation pauses, notifies the right person with the relevant context, and waits for their input before continuing. The automated parts still run automatically. The human steps are just cleaner and better-informed than they were before.

Q: How long does a typical build take?
A: A simple automation connecting two tools with a straightforward trigger and action typically takes a few weeks depending on scope — we'll confirm an exact timeline after the audit. A multi-step process involving several systems and conditional logic takes longer. We give you a timeline during scoping, and it's included in the fixed-price quote.

Q: What if our process changes after the automation is built?
A: Processes change. We document everything clearly so you understand what was built and why. Small changes: an extra notification, a new field: are usually quick to make. If the process changes significantly, we scope it as a new build or an extension.

Q: How do we know it's actually running correctly?
A: Every automation produces a run log. You can see every time it triggered, what data it processed, and what the output was. We review this with you in the first weeks and set up a monitoring alert for any failures.
