## Section: The problem

Label: What disconnected tools actually cost you

When your systems don't talk to each other, a human becomes the connection. Someone copies the new lead from the form into the CRM. Someone updates the job status in the project tool. Someone pulls the data from three places to build the weekly report. That's not a people problem: it's a systems problem.

The real cost isn't just the time. It's the errors. Every manual transfer is a chance for data to be entered wrong, fields to be left blank, or a step to be skipped entirely. And when deals fall through the cracks, you rarely know which part of the process dropped them.

Bad CRM data compounds over time. Duplicate records, outdated contacts, missing information: it quietly degrades the quality of every decision that relies on it.

## Section: What we build

Label: What we build

We audit the tools you use and map how information should move between them. Then we build the integrations that make it move automatically.

A new lead comes in through your website form and lands directly in your CRM with the right fields populated, the right pipeline stage set, and the first follow-up task created. A deal closes and your project management tool gets the new project, your accounting software gets the invoice trigger, and your onboarding sequence fires. A contact goes quiet for 90 days and your CRM flags it without anyone having to run a report.

We work with the tools you already have. HubSpot, GoHighLevel, Pipedrive, Salesforce, Notion, Airtable, Xero, QuickBooks, Google Workspace, and most others that have an API or native integration. If a tool doesn't connect cleanly, we'll tell you that before we start.

We also clean and structure your existing CRM data as part of the setup. There's no point building clean integrations on top of dirty data.

## Section: How it works

Label: From fragmented tools to a connected system

01: Systems audit
We map every tool you use, what data lives in it, and where the manual handoffs are today. This usually surfaces problems you didn't know were costing you.

02: Integration design
We design the data flows: what triggers what, what maps to what, and where a human decision needs to stay in the loop. You review and approve the design before we build.

03: Build and clean
We build the integrations and clean your existing CRM data as part of the same process. Duplicates removed, missing fields filled where possible, pipelines structured correctly.

04: Test and hand over
We test every data flow with real scenarios before going live. We then document the integrations clearly so you understand what's running in your own system.

## Section: FAQ

Q: We already have some Zapier automations. Is this different?
A: It depends on what they're doing. Simple Zapier automations are fine for basic triggers and actions. Where they fall short is complex conditional logic, error handling, bulk data operations, and anything that needs to be reliable at scale. We often extend or replace Zapier automations as part of a broader integration build. Sometimes the existing ones are fine and we leave them alone.

Q: Our CRM data is a mess. Does that need to be fixed first?
A: We include a data cleanup as part of the integration build. There's no point building clean automations on top of inaccurate data, so we handle both together. The state of your data doesn't stop us starting: it just becomes part of the scope.

Q: Can you work with our specific CRM?
A: Probably yes. We've worked with HubSpot, GoHighLevel, Pipedrive, Salesforce, and several others. The clearest way to check is on the audit call: you tell us your stack and we tell you what's connectable and what isn't.

Q: What if we change tools in future?
A: We document everything we build so another developer: or us: can update the integrations when your stack changes. Integration builds aren't meant to lock you into working with us forever.

Q: How do we know the data is flowing correctly after launch?
A: Every integration produces a run log. You can see what triggered, what data moved, and what the output was. We also set up monitoring alerts so any failure notifies you immediately rather than going unnoticed.

Q: Do you train our team on the new setup?
A: Yes. We include a handover session where we walk your team through what's been built, what they can expect the system to do, and what to do if something looks wrong. The documentation we provide is written for your team, not for developers.
